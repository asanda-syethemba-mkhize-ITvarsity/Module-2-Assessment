GEMSBOKS

The Gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is the common name given to the largest and and most well known of the 3 species of Oryx. There are two types of Gemsbok, a northern and a southern variety. The only difference between them is that the nothern variety have black fringes on their ears while the southern variety havelonger horns and their ears are more rounded in shape. Gemsboks stands around 4 feet 7 inches at shoulder height and weight 230-240 kilograms (females weight slightly less than males at round 200-210 kilograns).

Gemsbok has strike black and white markings on the face and legs and black side stripes and has thick, horse like neck with a short mane, a compact, mascular body with a long flowing horse-like tail that reaches to their hocks.

Germsboks are herbivore and can be found on grassland or savannas and on flat or hill country.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You will find the gemsboks at pen A20, right next to the monkey trail.
